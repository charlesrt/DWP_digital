Digital and technology jobs at the UK Department for Work and Pensions.

Transforming DWP

DWP delivers public services that millions of people rely on. We touch the lives of almost everyone in the UK at some point. We change lives by lifting people out of poverty and letting them live independently.

People now expect to find and use our services digitally. To meet this challenge we're changing how we work, designing simpler, clearer and faster services that put customers first.

Services that change people's lives.

At the same time, we're changing the welfare system in the UK.

We're simplifying the benefits system through Universal Credit. We're increasing opportunities for disabled people through Personal Independence Payments. And we're making sure people can enjoy retirement by supporting pension savings and changing how the State Pension works.

Our technology challenge

DWP's technology challenge is one of the largest in the UK today. Our IT systems manage 7.35m benefit claims each year, in the process paying £165 billion in benefits and pensions, including £1.2 billion in child maintenance.

We now need experts in digital design, technology and programming languages, to build reusable applications, platforms and libraries.

Building agile services, putting user needs first.

We want digital technology in DWP to be a leading example of agile delivery. We want our people to be experts, motivated by the challenge of working on large-scale user-centred applications running on massive platforms.

It's an exciting time to join us.
