About us Helping people through work, saving and support

We have a very important purpose – helping people lift themselves out of poverty and stay out of poverty.

We design and deliver public services that millions of people rely on. We transform lives by helping the most disadvantaged people to turn their lives around. Last year we helped 105,000 families turn their lives around through the troubled Families Programme.

We touch almost every citizen in the UK at some point in their lives. From recognising the importance of family in provided the foundations of every child’s life, by encouraging work – and making it pay, by enabling work and independence for those with disabilities or ill-health and by promoting firm foundations for retirement – helping those in the later stages of life to maintain their standard of living.

We are the largest Government Department, delivering once in a lifetime welfare reform

Last year we helped 2.6m people off Jobseeker's Allowance and paid 22m people £167.6bn in benefits and pensions

We saw a record 31.1m people in employment, and helped more than 32,400 disabled people find and stay in work
